# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy - SpendOptic

---

## 1. Precise Target User
Our primary target buyer is the **Fractional CFO, VP of Finance, or Head of Engineering / CTO** at **Series B and Seed stage technology startups (team size: 10 to 150 employees)**.

At this phase:
- **Finance Leaders** are under pressure to cut SaaS software spend because of tightening growth markets, yet they lack the engineering context to evaluate which AI developer tools (Cursor vs. Copilot) are active or duplicative.
- **Engineering Managers** buy tools in silos, leading to severe license leakage (e.g. paying for seat counts exceeding total active developer counts).
- **The Pain Point**: AI tools are billed as small, easily reimbursable items, adding up to thousands of dollars in hidden corporate overhead.

---

## 2. Where They Search & What They Read
Before utilizing SpendOptic, these buyers are typically reading:
1. **Fractional CFO Communities & Blogs**: e.g., CFO Connect, OnlyCFO newsletter, or FPP (Finance for Startup Founders) publications.
2. **Productivity Forums**: Discussions around dev tool productivity (such as *Is Cursor really worth the $20 upgrade over Copilot?*) on Hacker News and specialized developer circulars.
3. **Engineering Management lists**: Lead Dev blogs and newsletters analyzing modern cloud cost optimizations.

---

## 3. Specific Distribution Channels
We will distribute directly into high-intent professional communities:
- **Subreddits**: `r/startups` (for founders looking to trim cash burn), `r/CFO` (finance leaders looking for quick auditing tools), and `r/svele` (for software leaders).
- **Slack & Discord Spaces**: Startup Partners Slack, CFO Club, Fractional CFO Collective, and various local technology hubs on Discord.
- **Social Media (X/Twitter)**: Building "in public" on X, tagging specialized lists (such as #StartupOptimization lists) and tech finance commentators (e.g., OnlyCFO).

---

## 4. Plan to Get Core 100 Users in 30 Days (On a $0 Budget)

Our action roadmap is split into discrete chronological sprints:

### Week 1: Direct Partner Alpha Onboarding (Users 1-15)
- Reach out to 15 Fractional CFOs on LinkedIn offering SpendOptic as a free audit white-glove service they can present to their startup clients.
- Gather feedback and refine the deterministic calculators based on their real bills.

### Week 2: Content-Led Launch on Hacker News & Product Hunt (Users 16-50)
- Publish a professional text-only blog/markdown post on Hacker News titled: *"Show HN: An open-source, no-login AI tool spend auditor for startups"*.
- Highlight our PRICING_DATA.md transparency and emphasize the no-login, trust-first model.

### Week 3: High-Intent Community Seeding (Users 51-80)
- Join specialized subreddits (`r/startups`, `r/svele`) and present real compiled lookup reports (PII stripped). For instance, posting a Case Study on *"How a 40-person tech startup saved $12,000/yr by removing redundant Copilot accounts after Cursor migrations"*.

### Week 4: The Viral Shareable Loop (Users 81-100+)
- Prompt users who run audits on our homepage to share their reports. Since each audit generates a neat public URL with Open Graph preview cards showing exact optimized savings on Twitter, this naturally pulls in peer startup leaders in high-growth networks.

---

## 5. The Unfair Distribution Channel
Our unfair advantage comes from **Fractional CFO partnerships**. A single fractional CFO often manages the finances and licensing grids for **5 to 15 separate technology startups** simultaneously. 
By turning SpendOptic into a "single-click evaluation kit" that CFOs can use for free to prove immediate value to corporate founders, we turn 1 champion into 10-15 high-value corporate leads without paying a dime in marketing CAC.
Our estimated Week-1 alpha traction numbers aim for **12 partner audits, discovering over $24,000 in total annualized AI spend runaways**.
